Preventive Measures

Stay safe with recommended precautions based on local disease outbreaks.

Disease-Specific Precautions

Dengue

  • • Remove standing water from containers, flower pots, and tires.
  • • Use mosquito repellent and wear long sleeved clothing.
  • • Install mosquito nets on windows and doors.
  • • Ensure proper waste disposal to prevent mosquito breeding.

Malaria

  • • Sleep under insecticide-treated bed nets (ITNs).
  • • Take anti-malarial medication if traveling to high-risk areas.
  • • Wear protective clothing during dusk and dawn (peak mosquito hours).
  • • Eliminate mosquito breeding sites around your home.

Typhoid

  • • Drink only boiled or bottled water.
  • • Wash hands thoroughly before eating and after using the toilet.
  • • Get vaccinated against typhoid if in high-risk areas.
  • • Avoid raw vegetables and fruits unless washed with safe water.

Cholera

  • • Drink safe, treated water only; purify when necessary.
  • • Practice good hand hygiene with soap and water frequently.
  • • Eat thoroughly cooked food while it's hot; avoid raw or undercooked seafood.
  • • Use proper sanitation facilities and safe waste disposal.

Pneumonia

  • • Get the annual flu shot and the pneumococcal vaccine.
  • • Practice good respiratory etiquette (cover coughs/sneezes).
  • • Avoid smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke.
  • • Maintain distance from people who are ill.
